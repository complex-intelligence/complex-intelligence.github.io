About

소개

The Complex Intelligence Research Group (복합지능연구회), founded in February 2025, is an interdisciplinary research group dedicated to understanding the significance of the civilizational transformation brought about by AI and exploring its direction.

Drawing on the perspectives of philosophy of technology, phenomenology, hermeneutics, theology, and cognitive science, the group examines the similarities and differences between human and artificial intelligence, asking whether each possesses an irreducible domain unique to itself. Can AI truly think as humans do? Are the acts of interpretation and understanding possible for machines? What ontological status do technical objects hold within human civilization? — Starting from Erik Larson's critique of AI, through the intersection of hermeneutic traditions and AI, theological and religious reflections, Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics and Simondon's theory of technical objects, to Kissinger's vision of world order in the age of AI, the group pursues these questions on multiple levels, combining theoretical inquiry with hands-on technological practice.

Through this inquiry, we seek to move beyond attitudes that either reject or mythologize AI, and to explore how humans and AI can coexist meaningfully on this shared planet in harmony — that is, to explore the possibility of co-evolution toward a complex intelligence that integrates human and artificial intelligence.
